Daily Session Design

Each session (15‚Äì30 min) begins with a Morning Brief ‚Äî the world presents 3 pending events: a meeting, a social invitation, and an emergency. Players choose their order, and each interaction is fully voice-driven with no text fallback. The session ends with a Day Review screen showing how every spoken word translated into real outcomes: salary delta, relationship health, and reputation shift visualized as a timeline of consequence.

Voice Evaluation Engine

The AI evaluates five dimensions simultaneously: Tone (warmth, assertiveness, anxiety), Clarity (sentence structure, filler words), Confidence (pace, volume, hesitation patterns), Vocabulary (breadth, appropriateness to context), and Relevance (did you actually answer the question?). Characters react to all five simultaneously, creating emergent, realistic social dynamics.

Character Memory Architecture

Every character maintains a Vector Memory Store ‚Äî a compressed semantic representation of all past interactions. Characters don't memorize exact words but remember emotional trajectories, trust arc, and recurring patterns. For example, if a player historically hesitates before answering salary questions, the AI recruiter will probe on compensation topics specifically. Memory decays realistically: casual contacts forget faster than close mentors. Relationship graphs update after every session, and the world simulation reflects who remembers you and how.
